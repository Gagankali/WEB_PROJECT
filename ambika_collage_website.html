Indian culture

About the Culture

12 DECEMBER MAITHILI SHARAN GUPT (1886 to 1964) Born on 03 August 1886, Maithili Sharan Gupt was one of India’s most famous modern Hindi poets. While most other poets used the Braj Bhasha dialect (the dominant language at that time), Gupt wrote in the Khari Boli dialect and is considered to be the pioneer of Khari Boli poetry. Most of Gupt’s work revolved around the Hindu epics: The Mahabharata and The Ramayana. The poet's most influential work, Rang Mein Bhang was published in the year 1910. In 1912, Gandhiji gave him the title of Rashtra Kavi for his book of nationalist poems, Bharat Bharati. It is said that when he became a Rajya Sabha MP, he used to express his opinions in poetry form in Parliament. He was honoured with the Padma Bhushan by the Govt. of India in 1954. Maithili Sharn Gupt passed away on 12 December 1964
